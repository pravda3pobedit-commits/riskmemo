# Future Scope

Keep the public MVP simple. The first product is:

> Manual review of suspicious messages, vacancies, and offers.

Do not lead with HR, diagnostics, personality judgment, hidden defects, or leadership potential. Those directions may be explored later only if there is demand and a safer framing.

## Possible Serious Extensions

### Communication Risk Review

For business conversations, partnerships, negotiations, and client screening.

Safe language:

- Communication risk review.
- Counterparty message review.
- Psycholinguistic communication audit.
- Behavioral risk markers in written communication.

Avoid:

- "I detect fraudsters."
- "I see hidden vices."
- "This person is mentally abnormal."

### HR / Hiring Adjacent

High risk. Do not launch this in the MVP.

Possible future framing:

- Interview communication review.
- Written communication consistency review.
- Soft-skill signal review from self-written answers.

Guardrails:

- No protected-class inference.
- No psychiatric or medical claims.
- No automated hiring decision.
- No "leadership potential" claims unless there is a validated process and clear disclaimers.

### Nonverbal / Photo / Video

Not in the MVP.

If explored later, frame as:

- Nonverbal communication markers.
- Presentation and trust-signal review.

Avoid claiming that a photo reveals character, morality, pathology, or guaranteed deception.

## Current Public Positioning

Use simple words:

- What looks risky?
- What should I verify?
- What can I safely reply?
- What should I avoid sending until verified?

The serious layer can remain behind the scenes as a method, not the headline.
